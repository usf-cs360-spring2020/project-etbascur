Questions for this visualization

With this visualization I want to see: What is the increase or decrease of the subcategories in the calls made to the police department overall? What is the increase or decrease of the subcategory in the calls made to the police department by neighborhood? Is there any difference in a particular type of subcategory that has increased more due to the shelter in place order? (speculative) Have any of these shifted? (For example: car larceny was higher in tourist areas).

Answers from this visualizations

Overall comparing the same time range from last year to this year, there are considerably less calls about any particular subcategory to the police. However there are some subcategories that seem to be on the rise. Among them fire calls and arson. There is still more to investigate.

Encoding

The visualization above has two parts, currently they are not connected the way they are intended.

Map

The encoding on the side with the map has the different subcategories divided by color, and the sum of cases in this location by size. Each circle is a location where a case has taken place. They are also divided by year, either 2019 or 2020.

Barchart

For the Barchart the Y-axis contains all the subcategories. The X-axis has a percentage range from -100% to +130% approximately. Each subcategory has a horizontal bar starting at 0 and growing towards either the negative or the positive direction. The chart compares the year 2019 to the year 2020 and gets the percentage difference between the two.

Interactivity

The planned interactivity makes the visualizations much more organized than what is currently on the beta. There will be a dropdown tab to choose which neighborhood will be shown both on the map and the bar chart, there will also be an 'on-click' event that will highlight a particular subcategory in both the map and the bar chart, while making non-matching subcategories disappear on the map.
Brushing by year will also be implemented so as to be able to compare the previous year to this year. When the cursor hovers over a point in the map, a tooltip with relevant information will appear. I'm also considering implementing a comparison of a particular subcategory by neighborhood, on the map it would show a particular subcategory on the entire map, and in the bar chart it would change the y-axis from subcategory to neighborhood.
